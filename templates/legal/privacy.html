Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2025-08-19

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy (the “Policy”) explains how the ComeBack Admin panel (the “Service”) collects, uses, discloses, retains, and protects information. By using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to this Policy.

2. Scope and roles

The Service is an internal, Django-based administrative tool used by authorized personnel to manage AR content (videos with GPS coordinates), synchronize data with Firebase Realtime Database and Firebase Storage, review payments, and generate one-time passwords (OTP) for cash payments. User roles include “Administrator” and “Cashier.” Your organization acts as the data controller for information processed through the Service, and may engage third-party processors (e.g., Firebase by Google) to support functionality.

3. Definitions

  • “Personal data” means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (where applicable under local law).
  • “Processing” means any operation performed on information, such as collection, storage, use, disclosure, or deletion.
  • “Controller/Processor” have the meanings assigned under applicable data protection law (e.g., GDPR).

4. Categories of information

Depending on your role and use, we may process the following:

  • Account data: username (login), role (Administrator/Cashier), internal identifiers. Passwords are stored as cryptographic hashes and are never stored in plain text.
  • Operational data: video metadata (title, description, GPS coordinates), subscription settings (price, duration, currency), OTP codes (value, quantity, status, timestamps, created by), payment records (order ID, amount, currency, status, timestamps, description where provided, subscription duration, OTP/cash flag).
  • Content and media: uploaded video files and associated descriptions; public storage URLs where applicable.
  • Technical data: IP address, device/browser information, timestamps, session identifiers, error events, audit trails, application and server logs.
  • Integration data: identifiers and metadata required to synchronize with Firebase services.
  • Cookies and sessions: identifiers strictly necessary for authentication and session continuity.

5. Sources of information

  • Information you provide (e.g., uploading videos, creating OTP codes).
  • Information generated by the Service (e.g., audit logs, IDs, timestamps).
  • Information synchronized with third-party providers to enable functionality (e.g., Firebase).

6. Purposes of processing

  • Provide, operate, and secure the Service, including role-based access control.
  • Audit, log, and monitor actions for security and compliance.
  • Synchronize content and operational data with Firebase (Realtime Database and Storage).
  • Generate and track OTP codes for cash payments and present payment statistics.
  • Improve performance, reliability, and usability, and troubleshoot issues.

7. Legal bases

Where required by law, processing is carried out based on: (a) performance of a contract or assigned duties; (b) legitimate interests in operating and securing the Service; (c) compliance with legal obligations (e.g., record-keeping); and, where applicable, (d) consent.

8. Retention

  • Account data: retained for the life of the account and up to 12 months after deactivation, unless internal policy requires otherwise.
  • Logs and technical data: retained for at least 6 months, then archived or deleted per internal policy.
  • Content and payment records: retained per accounting, audit, operational, and legal requirements.

9. Disclosures and recipients

  • Internal: authorized personnel (Administrators, Cashiers) on a need-to-know basis.
  • Service providers: Firebase (Google) for hosting, realtime database, and storage; processing is subject to their terms and applicable law.
  • Legal and safety: where required by law, regulation, court order, or to protect rights, safety, or the integrity of the Service.

10. International transfers

Use of Firebase may involve international transfers. Providers may rely on appropriate safeguards (e.g., standard contractual clauses). By using the Service, you acknowledge such transfers may occur to deliver core functionality.

11. Security measures

We employ organizational and technical measures, including authentication, role-based access controls, principle of least privilege, monitoring, and—where applicable—encrypted transport. No method is entirely secure; residual risk remains.

12. Cookies and similar technologies

We use session cookies and similar identifiers strictly necessary for authentication and session continuity. Blocking these may impair functionality.
